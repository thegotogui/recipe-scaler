Roasted Tomato Soup

Starter
⏱ 50 min 👤 4 servings

Slow-roasted plum tomatoes blended with garlic confit and a swirl of cream. Deep, sweet, and effortlessly comforting.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg plum tomatoes, halved
  • 1 whole head of garlic
  • 1 medium onion, roughly chopped
  • 30 ml olive oil
  • 600 ml vegetable stock
  • 60 ml double cream
  • 5 g fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 200 °C. Arrange the tomatoes cut-side up on a baking tray. Slice the top off the garlic head to expose the cloves. Drizzle everything with olive oil and season generously. Roast for 35 minutes until caramelised.
  2. Squeeze the roasted garlic cloves out of their skins into a large saucepan. Add the onion and sauté over medium heat for 5 minutes until soft.
  3. Add the roasted tomatoes and all their juices to the pan along with the stock. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Blend until smooth using a stick blender. Pass through a sieve for an extra-smooth finish if desired.
  5. Stir in the cream, adjust seasoning, and serve with a drizzle of oil and fresh thyme.

Braised Short Ribs

Main
⏱ 3 hr 30 min 👤 4 servings

Fall-off-the-bone beef short ribs braised low and slow in red wine and stock with aromatic vegetables and fresh thyme.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 kg bone-in beef short ribs
  • 30 ml vegetable oil
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 30 g tomato paste
  • 375 ml dry red wine
  • 500 ml beef stock
  • 5 g fresh thyme sprigs
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and black pepper

Method

  1. Season the ribs generously on all sides. Heat the oil in a large Dutch oven over high heat and sear the ribs in batches until well-browned, about 3–4 minutes per side. Set aside.
  2. Reduce heat to medium. Add the onion, carrots, and celery and cook for 5 minutes until softened. Add the garlic and tomato paste and stir for 1 minute.
  3. Pour in the red wine and scrape up any browned bits from the base. Simmer for 5 minutes, then add the stock, thyme, and bay leaves.
  4. Return the ribs to the pot, meat-side down. Bring to a simmer, cover, and transfer to a 150 °C oven for 3 hours.
  5. Remove the ribs carefully. Strain and reduce the braising liquid on the hob until slightly syrupy. Spoon over the ribs to serve.

Lemon Ricotta Pasta

Main
⏱ 20 min 👤 2 servings

A quick weeknight dish of pasta tossed with whipped ricotta, bright lemon zest, black pepper, and a handful of fresh basil.

Ingredients

  • 200 g dried spaghetti or linguine
  • 200 g full-fat ricotta
  • 1 lemon, zested and juiced
  • 40 g Parmesan, finely grated, plus extra to serve
  • 30 ml extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 garlic clove, finely grated
  • 10 g fresh basil leaves
  • Salt and plenty of black pepper

Method

  1. Cook the pasta in generously salted boiling water according to pack instructions. Reserve 120 ml of pasta water before draining.
  2. While the pasta cooks, whisk together the ricotta, lemon zest, lemon juice, grated garlic, and olive oil until smooth. Season well.
  3. Stir in the Parmesan.
  4. Drain the pasta and immediately toss with the ricotta sauce, adding pasta water a splash at a time until you have a loose, silky coating.
  5. Serve at once topped with torn basil, extra Parmesan, and a crack of black pepper.

Flourless Chocolate Torte

Dessert
⏱ 1 hr 👤 8 servings

An intensely rich, fudgy torte made with dark chocolate and eggs — naturally gluten-free, best served with crème fraîche.

Ingredients

  • 200 g dark chocolate (70%), chopped
  • 150 g unsalted butter, cubed
  • 180 g caster sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 30 g unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 2 g fine salt
  • 5 ml vanilla extract
  • Crème fraîche, to serve

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 175 °C. Grease a 22 cm springform tin and line the base with baking paper.
  2. Melt the chocolate and butter together in a heatproof bowl over barely simmering water, stirring until smooth. Cool for 5 minutes.
  3. Whisk the sugar into the chocolate mixture, then beat in the eggs one at a time until fully incorporated.
  4. Sift in the cocoa and salt, add the vanilla, and fold until just combined.
  5. Pour into the prepared tin and bake for 22–25 minutes until the edges are set but the centre has a slight wobble. Cool completely before slicing.

Miso Glazed Aubergine

Side
⏱ 35 min 👤 4 servings

Charred aubergine halves lacquered with white miso, mirin, and sesame — a satisfying vegetarian centrepiece.

Ingredients

  • 2 medium aubergines, halved lengthways
  • 45 g white (shiro) miso paste
  • 30 ml mirin
  • 15 ml sake or dry sherry
  • 10 g caster sugar
  • 15 ml sesame oil
  • 10 g sesame seeds, toasted
  • 3 spring onions, thinly sliced

Method

  1. Score the cut surface of each aubergine half in a crosshatch pattern without piercing the skin. Brush with sesame oil and place cut-side down on a hot griddle for 5 minutes until charred.
  2. Flip and cook skin-side down for a further 5 minutes. Transfer to a baking tray, cut-side up.
  3. Whisk together the miso, mirin, sake, and sugar until smooth. Spoon generously over the cut surface of each half.
  4. Place under a hot grill for 5–7 minutes until the glaze is bubbling and caramelised in spots.
  5. Scatter with sesame seeds and spring onions. Serve immediately.

Classic Vinaigrette

Sauce
⏱ 5 min 👤 6 servings

A properly emulsified Dijon vinaigrette — the one you'll make on autopilot once you've done it three times.

Ingredients

  • 10 g Dijon mustard
  • 30 ml red wine vinegar
  • 90 ml extra virgin olive oil
  • 3 g honey
  • 1 small garlic clove, finely grated
  • 2 g fine salt
  • 1 g freshly ground black pepper

Method

  1. Combine the mustard, vinegar, honey, garlic, salt, and pepper in a small bowl or jar and mix until combined.
  2. While whisking constantly, drizzle in the olive oil in a slow, steady stream to form a smooth, emulsified dressing.
  3. Taste and adjust — more vinegar for sharpness, a touch more honey for balance. Store in a sealed jar in the fridge for up to 1 week. Shake well before using.
